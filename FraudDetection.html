5 Ways to Spot a Scam in 30 Seconds

Scammers don’t hack computers. They hack urgency.

If a message makes your heart rate jump, pause before you act.

🚩 Red Flag #1: Urgency + Fear

“Your account will be suspended.” “Unusual activity detected.” “Act now.”

Scammers impersonate major companies to create panic.

🚩 Red Flag #2: Call This Number

If an email tells you to call a support number inside the message, don’t.

Go directly to the official website instead.

🚩 Red Flag #3: Install Support Software

No legitimate company will cold-call you and ask for remote access to your computer.

🚩 Red Flag #4: Slightly Wrong Email Address

Check the sender carefully. Small differences matter.

🚩 Red Flag #5: Strange Payment Methods

Gift cards, crypto, or wire transfers to fix a “problem” are always scams.

The 30-Second Scam Check

  • Is this trying to scare me?
  • Is it asking me to call a number in the message?
  • Is it asking me to install software?
  • Is the sender address slightly off?
  • Is the payment method unusual?

If two or more are yes → stop and verify directly through the official website.
